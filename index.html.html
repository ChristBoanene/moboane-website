Thought Leadership & Legal Updates

September 2025

OHADA Reforms and Corporate Governance

Recent updates to OHADA regulations present new opportunities for corporate structuring in Cameroon and across the CEMAC region. Companies should review their governance frameworks to ensure compliance with updated requirements for board composition, shareholder rights, and financial reporting. The reforms also introduce enhanced protections for minority shareholders and clearer guidelines for related-party transactions.

August 2025

Tax Optimization for Cross-Border Transactions

With evolving international tax standards and increased scrutiny on transfer pricing, businesses operating across multiple jurisdictions must adopt strategic approaches to tax planning that balance efficiency with regulatory compliance. This includes careful structuring of intercompany transactions and comprehensive documentation requirements.

July 2025

Due Diligence in Emerging Markets: Key Considerations

Conducting thorough due diligence in African markets requires attention to unique regulatory environments, land tenure systems, and informal business practices. Investors should focus not only on financial and legal review but also on operational realities, political risk assessment, and cultural factors that may impact business success. Environmental and social governance considerations are becoming increasingly important to both local regulators and international investors.

June 2025

Corporate Formation Strategies for Startups

Entrepreneurs launching businesses in Cameroon face critical choices regarding entity structure, capitalization, and shareholder arrangements. The decision between SARL and SA structures has significant implications for governance, fundraising capacity, and exit planning. Early-stage companies should also consider employee equity arrangements, intellectual property ownership, and regulatory compliance from inception to avoid costly restructuring later.

May 2025

Competition Law Enforcement in CEMAC

CEMAC's regional competition framework is evolving, with increased enforcement activity and significant penalties for non-compliance. Companies engaging in mergers, joint ventures, or holding significant market positions should be aware of notification obligations, prohibited practices, and the potential for dawn raids. The regional commission is developing more sophisticated analytical approaches similar to those used in mature jurisdictions.
